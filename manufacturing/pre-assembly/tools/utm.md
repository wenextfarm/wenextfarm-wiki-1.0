# Step 1: Attach pogo pins to the PCB

Attach 12 **pogo pins** to the **UTM PCB** using 24 **jam nuts** (2 per pogo pin).

![utm pcb with pogo pins and connectors](_images/utm_pcb_with_pogo_pins_and_connectors.png =700px)

# Step 2: Mount the PCB and magnets

Insert the **UTM PCB** into the [UTM](UTM). It is keyed, and should only fit in one orientation.

![utm with pcb inserted](_images/utm_with_pcb_inserted.png =700px)

Slide a **ring magnet** onto an [M5 x 16mm screw](M5 x 16mm screw). The magnet's `South` face should be butted against the screw head.

![magnet and screw](_images/magnet_and_screw.png =700px)

Fasten the PCB in the UTM with the ring magnet and screw. Repeat with two more magnets and screws.

> **ℹ️ Ensure all magnets face the same direction:** All magnets must be mounted in the same magnetic orientation. The `North` faces should be butted up against the PCB while the `South` faces should butted against the screw heads.

![utm with pcb attached with magnets and screws](_images/utm_with_pcb_attached_with_magnets_and_screws.png =700px)

# Step 3: Add o-rings

Slide three **x-profile o-rings** onto the [UTM](UTM).

![utm with o-rings](_images/utm_with_o-rings.png =700px)

# Step 4: Add barbs

Screw three **barbs** into the top of the [UTM](UTM).

![utm with barbs](_images/utm_with_barbs.png =700px)

# Step 5: Pack

Pack the **pre-assembled UTM** into the **UTM box**.
