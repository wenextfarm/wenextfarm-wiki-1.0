# Step 1: Prepare the cable carrier

Remove all of the snap-in **tabs** from the **z-axis cable carrier**. The z-axis cable carrier is the shortest cable carrier in the kit.

![Remove the tabs](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/remove_z_tabs_1.png =700x)
![Remove the tabs](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/remove_z_tabs_2.png =700x)

# Step 2: Orient the cable carrier

There is only one orientation that the **z-axis cable carrier** mounts to the **cross-slide** and **z-axis**, and it is determined by the orientation of the **end pieces**. Inspect the images below to see how the cable carrier will be mounted, but do not attach it at this time.

![Z cable carrier orientation (z-axis end)](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/z_cable_carrier_orientation_1.png =700x)
![Z cable carrier orientation (cross-slide end)](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/z_cable_carrier_orientation_2.png =700x)

# Step 3: Lay everything into the cable carrier

Keeping the mounting orientation of the cable carrier in mind, lay the following items into the open cable carrier. The **cross-slide end** of all items should be sticking out from the **cross-slide end** of the cable carrier by approximately **5cm**. This will leave the appropriate remaining length of each item sticking out from the z-axis end of the cable carrier.

|Item|Cross-slide end description|
|----|---------------------------|
|Z-axis [water tube](water tube) (0.95m long)|Either
|Z-axis [UTM cable](UTM cable) (1.0m long)|End with the 90-degree connector
|[Camera](Camera) (1.0m long)|End with the 90-degree connector
|[Z vacuum pump cable](Z vacuum pump cable) (1.0m long)|End with the 90-degree connector
|ZZ [motor cable](motor cable) (1.8m long)|End with the 90-degree connector
|ZZ [encoder cable](encoder cable) (1.8m long)|End with the 90-degree connector

> **ℹ️ Note:** 
> 
> The exact number of pins in each connector may vary from the images below depending on when your kit was manufactured.
{.is-info}


![Cables and tube organization](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/z_cable_carrier_organization_cross_slide_end.png =700x)
![Cables and tube organization](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/z_cable_carrier_organization.png =700x)

> **✅ Organization is important!:** 
> 
> When laying the cables and tube in the cable carrier, ensure that none of the items criss-cross over each other. If they do, you will have trouble snapping in the tabs and you risk compressing the tube and preventing water flow.
{.is-success}


# Step 4: Snap in some tabs

Snap in three **cable carrier tabs** at both ends of the cable carrier, and a handful more spread throughout the middle so that as you mount the assembly, the cables and tubing will stay in place. Do not snap in all of the tabs at this time because that will make it difficult to make adjustments if needed.

![Snap in some tabs](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/snap_in_some_z_cc_tabs.png =700x)

# Step 5: Mount the cable carrier

Carefully drape the cable carrier assembly into position. Attach the cross-slide end of the **cable carrier** to the **cross-slide** using two [M5 x 30mm screws](M5 x 30mm screws), [M5 flange locknuts](M5 flange locknuts), and the [cable carrier spacer block](cable carrier spacer block). The screws should thread firmly through the cable carrier end piece.

![Drape the cable carrier into position](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/drape_z_cc.png =700x)
![Mount the cable carrier to the cross-slide](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/mount_z_cc_to_cross_slide.png =700x)

Now attach the z-axis end of the **cable carrier** to the lowest [vertical cable carrier support](vertical cable carrier support) on the **z-axis** using two [M5 x 16mm screws](M5 x 16mm screws) and [M5 flange locknuts](M5 flange locknuts). The screws should thread firmly through the cable carrier end piece.

![mount the cable carrier to the z-axis](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/mount_the_cable_carrier_to_the_z-axis.png =700x)

# Step 6: Connect the z-axis motor and encoder

Feed the **ZZ motor and encoder cables** up through the slots in the [vertical cable carrier supports](vertical cable carrier supports), and then through the slot in the [z-axis motor mount](z-axis motor mount). Then connect the cables to the motor and encoder.

![Connect the z motor and encoder cables](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/connect_z_motor_cables_1.png =700x)
![Connect the z motor and encoder cables](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/connect_z_motor_cables_2.png =700x)

# Step 7: Add the vertical motor housing

Attach the [vertical motor housing](vertical motor housing) to the [z-axis extrusion](z-axis extrusion) using [M5 x 10mm screws](M5 x 10mm screws) and [tee nuts](tee nuts).

![Add the vertical motor housing](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/add_vertical_motor_housing.png =700x)

# Step 8: Snap in the remaining tabs

Once everything is situated well, snap-in the remaining **cable carrier tabs**, ensuring that you maintain organization of the cables and tube.

![Snap in the remaining tabs](/assembly/cables-and-tubing/_images/snap_in_remaining_z_cc_tabs.png =700x)

> **⚠️ Not easy to adjust:** 
> 
> Once you snap-in the remaining tabs, it will become impossible to adjust anything without removing the majority of the tabs first. Make sure everything is in place before you make a bunch of extra work for yourself.
{.is-warning}
